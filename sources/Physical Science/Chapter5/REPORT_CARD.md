**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 5 (Holt Science Spectrum, "The Periodic Table") covers the history and structure of the periodic table across three sections: 5.1 Organizing the Elements (Mendeleev's table — arrangement by atomic mass, periodic gaps as predictions, discovered elements confirming predictions; Moseley's revision to atomic number; periods and groups; valence electrons defined), 5.2 Exploring the Periodic Table (energy level filling, counting valence electrons from atomic number, predicting PT position from electron arrangement, ionization, cations, anions, reactivity trends, metals/nonmetals/semiconductors), and 5.3 Families of Elements (alkali metals, alkaline-earth metals, transition metals, halogens, noble gases, synthetic elements, plus hydrogen and carbon as special cases; mercury as an environmental context). **This is the primary delivery chapter for HS-PS1-1** — the valence electron → group pattern → property prediction chain is complete here. Ch4 built the electron structure scaffold; Ch5 is where students first use the periodic table as a predictive tool, which is exactly what HS-PS1-1 requires. The ionization and family reactivity content also lays the foundation for **HS-PS1-2** (Ch6–7 complete that PE). The Mendeleev/Moseley narrative in 5.1 carries a strong **NOS/SEP-6 thread** (model with predictive power; model revised when a better organizing principle was found). **Cross-chapter gap tracker:** PS3-3 (heat transfer) still absent — unchanged from Ch3/Ch4 finding. Physics insertions (PS2-2/2-3) remain structural.

The sub-deck pacing structure (5.1-A/B, 5.2-C/D, 5.3-A–D) mirrors the Ch4 pattern and should be preserved. **Findings worth acting on:**

1. **Test Q4 — likely key error.** Q4 states "As you move from left to right across the periodic table, elements become more metallic" — this is incorrect (metallic character decreases left to right). If D ("become more metallic") is the answer key's correct response, fix before use. This is the highest-priority error in the chapter test.
