**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 6 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Structure of Matter") covers chemical bonding across four sections: 6.1 Compounds and Molecules (chemical bond definition; molecular models — ball-and-stick, space-filling, structural formula; bond length and bond angle; key concept: chemical structure determines compound properties; ionic networks vs. separate molecules; hydrogen bonds; particle attraction and physical state), 6.2 Ionic and Covalent Bonding (why atoms bond = to fill outermost energy level; ionic bonding = electron transfer, metal → cation, nonmetal → anion, ionic networks, conductivity when melted; covalent bonding = electron sharing, single/double/triple bonds, polar vs. nonpolar covalent; metallic bonding = electrons delocalized across nuclei; polyatomic ions), 6.3 Compound Names and Formulas (naming ionic compounds: cation-anion order, -ide suffix for anion, charge balance = sum to zero, transition metal variable charges with Roman numeral method; naming covalent compounds: prefix system, PT left-to-right element order; empirical formula vs. molecular formula), and 6.4 Organic and Biochemical Compounds (organic compound: covalently bonded + contains carbon; hydrocarbons: alkanes = single bonds only, alkenes = at least one double bond; alcohols: hydroxyl -OH group; polymers/monomers; biochemical compounds: carbohydrates = C/H/O, proteins = amino acid chains, DNA = nucleotides, double helix). **This is the primary delivery chapter for HS-PS1-2** — the complete explanation arc from valence electrons to bond type to compound properties is first assembled here. Ch5 established that valence electrons determine group behavior; Ch6 is where students first explain WHY atoms bond (stability through a filled outer level) and HOW (ionic = one atom transfers electrons to another; covalent = atoms share electrons). That is exactly the HS-PS1-2 chain. **HS-PS1-1 continues** (extended, not primary) — PT position drives the ionic/covalent prediction (metals → cations; nonmetals → anions) and the covalent naming rule (left element names first). The 6.3 nomenclature section is a prerequisite skill without a primary PE but is required for all subsequent chemistry. The 6.4 organic section is a literacy/application extension; carbon's four-valence-electron versatility (4 bonds → enormous molecular variety) is a HS-PS1-1 thread, but 6.4 lacks a primary PE target. **Cross-chapter gap tracker:** PS3-3 (heat transfer) still absent — unchanged from prior chapters. HS-PS1-2 is now substantially delivered; Ch7 will complete it with chemical reactions.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 6 is the bonding chapter — **HS-PS1-2's primary delivery** — and the material is solid. The four-section arc from molecular models (6.1) through bonding mechanisms (6.2) to nomenclature (6.3) to organic applications (6.4) is logically ordered and well-scaffolded by the sub-deck pacing structure. The sub-deck system (6.1-A/B, 6.2-A/B/C/D, 6.3-A/B + standalone naming decks, 6.4-A/B/C) mirrors the Ch4/Ch5 pattern and should be preserved. The CFU coverage is strong: 2 CFUs for 6.1, 4 for 6.2, 4 for 6.3, 4 for 6.4. The biggest curricular gap is **SEP-6 absence** — all CFUs are recall/definition; none asks students to construct an explanation from bond principles. The CFU 6.2-A bolt-on (NaCl formation from valence electron reasoning) is the highest-priority intervention in the chapter and is the clearest HS-PS1-2 application task possible. The supplemental article on diamond vs. graphite is a high-value HS-PS1-2 real-world anchor that is already in the folder and underused. **The test (ExamView PDF) covers 6.1–6.3 only — 6.4 is not tested**; applied items on the test are strong (Q8, Q14–Q18). A possible content error in CFU 6.4-A Q8 (acetylene formula) should be verified. Version clutter is moderate: 4–5 near-dup main-deck versions plus the usual (1)/(2) sub-deck clutter; a misfiled Biology Kahoot should be checked and relocated.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **CFU 6.2-A bolt-on is the chapter's highest-priority CER item.** The NaCl formation explanation — Na has 1 valence electron, Cl has 7, so Na transfers 1 electron to Cl, both reach stable configuration, ionic bond forms — is the complete HS-PS1-2 application chain. No existing CFU in Ch6 asks this. Adding it to 6.2-A turns a recall CFU into the chapter's SEP-6 anchor.

2. **Diamond/graphite article is underused.** `How Atomic Bonds Create the Hardest, Softest, and Strangest Substances.docx` is already in the folder and is the best HS-PS1-2 real-world context in the chapter (same element, different bonding → different properties). Pair it with the bolt-on CER prompt: *"Diamond and graphite are both made entirely of carbon. Use bonding to explain why one is the hardest natural substance and the other is a soft lubricant."* This turns the article into a SEP-6 activity at essentially zero preparation cost.

3. **6.4 is not tested.** The only test in the chapter covers 6.1–6.3. Organic and biochemical content (alkanes/alkenes, polymers, carbohydrates/proteins/DNA) has no assessment beyond CFUs. If 6.4 is expected on the chapter final or a cumulative test, this is a gap to address when rebuilding the test.

4. **CFU 6.4-A Q8 — acetylene formula corrected.** Original text read "Acetylene has the formula of C₂H₆" — that is ethane. Fixed to **C₂H₂** (acetylene = ethyne). Molecular formula = C₂H₂, empirical = CH. *(Done 2026-06-11)*

5. **`Kahoot Review - Bio_.xlsx` renamed — content verified as PS Chemistry.** Questions cover organic compounds, combustion, redox/oxidation, electrolysis, displacement reactions, covalent bonds — all PS Ch6/Ch7 content. Renamed to `Kahoot Review - PS Ch6-7 Chemistry.xlsx`. *(Done 2026-06-11)*

6. **No CFU 6.3-C exists — this is fine.** The 6.3 CFU coverage is: A (ionic naming recall), B (covalent naming definitions), B2 (covalent naming applied practice), D (formula calculation practice). The C slot was never created, but the content is fully covered by B + B2 + D. Not a gap, just a naming quirk.

7. **[Priority CER note — Phase 3] CFU 6.2-A bolt-on + diamond/graphite article = the HS-PS1-2 synthesis pair.** The bolt-on makes HS-PS1-2 testable at application level for the first time in the course; the article makes it visible in the real world. Neither requires new materials — just adding one [short-answer] item and scheduling the article as a post-6.2 reading.
