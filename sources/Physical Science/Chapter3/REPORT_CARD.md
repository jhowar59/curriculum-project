**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 3 (Holt Science Spectrum, "States of Matter") is **this course's primary treatment of the energy PEs it owns**. Four sections: 3.1 Matter and Energy (kinetic theory, 3 states of matter, plasma, kinetic energy / temperature / thermal energy distinction), 3.2 Changes of State (melting/evaporation/boiling/sublimation/condensation/freezing, temperature plateau during phase changes, Laws of Conservation of Mass and Energy), 3.3 Fluids (pressure P=F/A, buoyant force, Archimedes' Principle, float/sink, Pascal's Principle and hydraulics, Bernoulli's Principle, viscosity), 3.4 Behavior of Gases (gas properties, Boyle's/Gay-Lussac's/Charles's Laws). The chapter maps to **HS-PS3-1** (mathematical representation of energy changes) and **HS-PS3-2** (models for kinetic energy of particles at the macroscale → temperature, thermal energy) via 3.1/3.2, and to **HS-PS2-2** (mathematics for pressure and force on objects; P=F/A) via 3.3. **Note on cross-chapter gap:** heat transfer (conduction, convection, radiation) is not taught in this chapter — see Finding 4.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 3 delivers the clearest treatment of PS3-2 (kinetic energy → temperature → thermal energy; phase-change plateaus) in the course so far, and 3.3 adds meaningful PS2-2 math (P=F/A, Pascal's Principle with a worked worksheet). The boiling lab is the right hands-on anchor. The two weak spots: the gas laws (3.4) have zero practice material, and the chapter never teaches heat transfer methods (conduction, convection, radiation), which PS3-3 needs. The deck clutter is the heaviest yet (three near-dup copies each of the two main full decks), and one clearly misfiled document needs to go.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **Gas laws CFU created (2026-06-11, per teacher).** `CFU 3.4.docx` written: Q1–4 recall (gas properties, one question per law with example), Q5 [short-answer] application (tire pressure drops overnight — name Gay-Lussac's Law + explain). If adding math later, P₁V₁=P₂V₂ (Boyle's) is the natural first calculation.

2. **The boiling lab is fully equipped — run it.** Hot plates confirmed in room (INVENTORY §2 updated; photo was missed in the original inventory pass). Portable Bunsen burners + ring stands also available as backup.

3. **Heat transfer (conduction, convection, radiation) is absent — PS3-3 gap to track.** The chapter gets students to "energy changes when matter changes state" but never teaches the *mechanisms* by which heat moves between objects. HS-PS3-3 ("design, build, and refine a device that works within given constraints to convert one form of energy into another form of energy") requires understanding heat transfer. Check whether it appears in another chapter (Ch4/Ch5 possibly), and flag it as a PS course-level gap if not.

4. **`Untitled document.docx` deleted (2026-06-11, per teacher).** Mars astrobiology summary — misfiled, no role in PS Ch3.

5. **CFU gap for 3.1-B** (the energy/temperature/thermal energy slides). No CFU exists for the second half of section 3.1 where the temperature vs. thermal energy distinction is drawn. The [short-answer] bolt-on for the 3.1-A CFU (coffee cup vs. full pot) addresses this — it's the most important conceptual distinction in the chapter.
