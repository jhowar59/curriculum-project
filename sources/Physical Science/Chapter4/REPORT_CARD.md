**Lab/activity column** cross-references the room's [INVENTORY.md](../INVENTORY.md) (§ = inventory section) to flag where a hands-on component exists or could plug in, and whether the equipment is on hand.
**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 4 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Atoms") covers the history and structure of atomic theory across three sections: 4.1 Development of Atomic Theory (Democritus, Dalton, Thomson's cathode-ray experiment/electron discovery, Rutherford's nuclear model), 4.2 Structure of Atoms (protons/neutrons/electrons, atomic number, mass number, isotopes, AMU, molar mass, Avogadro's number), and 4.3 Modern Atomic Theory (Bohr model, electron energy levels, subshells and orbitals, valence electrons, photons, ground and excited states). **This is a foundation-building chapter — it owns no full HS PE on its own.** Its primary contribution is the electron energy levels and valence electron concepts in 4.3, which are the direct prerequisite for **HS-PS1-1** (use periodic table to predict element properties based on outermost electron patterns); Ch5 formally delivers that PE. Section 4.1 carries a strong **NOS/SEP-6 thread** (atomic theory revised by evidence: Thomson's and Rutherford's experiments as models overturned by experimental data). The molar mass and Avogadro's number content in 4.2-D builds the quantitative scaffold for Ch7 stoichiometry (HS-PS1-7). **Cross-chapter gap tracker:** PS3-3 (heat transfer: conduction, convection, radiation) still absent — unchanged from Ch3 finding. Physics insertions (PS2-2/2-3) remain structural.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 4 has the most complete per-section CFU coverage of any PS chapter audited so far — 9 CFUs total (4.1×1, 4.2×4, 4.3×4), each matched to a corresponding sub-deck in an A–G (4.2) or A–D (4.3) pacing system. That deliberate daily-chunk structure is the chapter's strongest pedagogical feature and should be preserved. The "Building Isotopes" foam-ball lab is the clear hands-on anchor; foam balls and toothpicks are trivial to source if not already on hand. Every CFU is still recall-only — the pattern holds. The 4.3-B CFU (valence electrons) is the most important bolt-on target because HS-PS1-1 depends entirely on that concept (Phase 3 priority). Clutter is severe: 3–4 near-dup versions of Part 1, multiple near-dup variants of 4.2-C and 4.3-B/D, three text-parallel notes documents. Two AI-gen sub-day reading articles (Quantum Kitchen) have been moved to `../Articles/`; two naming repairs done (2026-06-11).

3. **`Untitled document(1).docx` renamed.** *(Done 2026-06-11)* Renamed to `Chapter 4 Study Guide.docx`. This is a genuine 9-Q review worksheet spanning all three sections — not junk, not the AI article.

4. **Two AI-enrichment article copies moved to `../Articles/`.** *(Done 2026-06-11, per teacher)* `Untitled document.docx` and `Quantum Kitchen_ The Weird Science of Microwave Physics.docx` are the same AI-generated microwave-physics article (different md5s, functionally identical). These are **sub-day reading materials** — students read, then answer a Google Quiz. Moved to `sources/Physical Science/Articles/` alongside "The Explosive History of Gunpowder.docx". Note: article topic (microwave physics / PS4 waves) better fits a physics unit than atomic theory; consider swapping out for an atomic theory sub-day reading.

5. **[Priority CER note — Phase 3] 4.3-B CFU bolt-on is the HS-PS1-1 linchpin.** The valence electron concept (4.3-B CFU Q6) is the exact content HS-PS1-1 is built on. Adding one [short-answer] item — "An element has 6 electrons. Describe how they fill the subshells and identify which are valence electrons." — makes Ch4 the point where the PS1-1 foundation is first tested at application level. Address this when building Ch4/Ch5 CFU revisions in Phase 3.

6. **4.2-D CFU has no math.** The moles/Avogadro CFU is pure recall (Q2/Q3 ask "how many particles" with no calculation). Adding one molar mass calculation (e.g., mass of 2.5 mol of a given element) converts this into the first quantitative practice for Ch7 stoichiometry.

7. **Skim test for key errors.** `Physical Science- Chapter 4 Test.docx` is AI-generated (156 paragraphs). Per the Ch1 pattern (key slip on Q12), the highest-risk Q are the molar mass/moles calculations (Q1 and similar) and any subshell-capacity items. Verify answer choices before using.
