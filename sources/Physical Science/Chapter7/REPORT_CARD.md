**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 7 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Chemical Reactions") covers four sections: 7.1 The Nature of Chemical Reactions (chemical reaction definition = two or more substances combine to form a new substance; four observable clues a reaction occurred; bonds break and atoms rearrange to form new bonds; reactants = substances going in; products = substances formed; chemical energy = energy stored in chemical bonds; photosynthesis = light energy → chemical energy stored in carbohydrate bonds; endothermic reactions = absorb more energy than they release, **products have MORE energy than reactants**; exothermic reactions = release more energy than they absorb, products have less energy than reactants; Law of Conservation of Energy = energy cannot be created or destroyed), 7.2 Chemical Equations (chemical equation = symbols showing relationship between reactants and products; balancing equations = making atom counts equal on both sides; coefficients; step-by-step balancing procedure; Law of Conservation of Mass; molar mass; mole ratios for quantitative calculations), 7.3 Reaction Types (synthesis = two or more substances join to form one; decomposition = one substance breaks into two; single-displacement = one atom/ion replaces another in a compound; double-displacement; electrolysis; polymerization = many units combine into long chain; combustion = organic compound + oxygen; incomplete combustion → CO and soot; free radicals = atom/group with one unpaired electron; oxidation-reduction (redox) = one substance loses electrons, another gains; reduced = gains electrons; oxidized = loses electrons), and 7.4 Reaction Rates and Equilibrium (factors affecting rate: temperature, concentration, surface area, particle mass/size/shape, pressure; temperature increases rate by increasing collision frequency and providing activation energy; concentration increases rate by providing more particles; surface area increases contact; larger particles react more slowly; higher pressure speeds gas reactions; catalyst = speeds up or slows down a reaction without being consumed; inhibitor = catalyst that slows; catalytic converter uses platinum catalyst to reduce exhaust emissions; enzymes = biological catalysts; substrate = reactant acted on by enzyme; active site = site on enzyme fitting only specific substrate; catalase breaks down hydrogen peroxide; reversible reactions occur in both directions). **This chapter completes the HS-PS1-2 delivery arc**: Ch6 established WHY atoms bond (outermost electron states) and HOW (ionic = electron transfer; covalent = electron sharing); Ch7 extends that to chemical reactions — bond breaking and forming, reaction classification (synthesis, decomposition, single-displacement = the specific types called out in the HS-PS1-2 clarification statement). **HS-PS1-7 is the primary new PE for Ch7**: Section 7.2 is explicitly about using mathematical representations (balanced equations, coefficients, mole ratios) to show atoms and mass are conserved. **HS-PS3-1 is touched but not fully delivered**: the endothermic/exothermic content in 7.1 is qualitative/definitional; the PS clarification for HS-PS3-1 emphasizes quantitative energy calculations, which the mole ratio work in 7.2-C begins to approach but does not complete.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 7 is the chemical reactions chapter — **HS-PS1-7's primary delivery** and the **completion of HS-PS1-2's reaction classification strand**. The four-section arc from reaction fundamentals (7.1) through equation notation and balancing (7.2) through reaction type classification (7.3) through rate factors (7.4) is logically ordered and well-scaffolded. The sub-deck system (7.1-A/B, 7.2-A/B/C, 7.3-A/B, 7.4-A[copy]/B/C) mirrors the Ch5/Ch6 pattern. CFU coverage is strong: 3 for 7.1 (including an IEP variant), 3 for 7.2, 3 for 7.3, 3 for 7.4. The biggest single issue is a **content error in the canonical 50-slide deck** (Sl9 says endothermic products have "less" energy — this is wrong; the sub-deck 7.1-B has the correct "more"). The biggest curriculum gap is the **test not including a balancing question** despite HS-PS1-7 being the chapter's primary new PE. The supplemental explosions article is high-value and underused.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **⚠ CONTENT ERROR — canonical `Chapter 7 Notes.pptx` Sl9**: "products of endothermic reactions have less energy than the reactants" is **wrong**. Endothermic reactions absorb energy; products have MORE energy than reactants. The correct version is in `Chapter 7.1 - B - Notes.pptx` Sl2. **Fix this before the next use of the full deck.** The `Chapter 7 - B Notes.pptx` old version has the same error and should be moved to Clutter.

2. **Test has no balancing question (HS-PS1-7 gap).** The ExamView test (20 Q) tests reaction type classification, rate factors, energy definitions, and molar mass (Q10), but no question asks a student to actually balance an equation. CFU 7.2-B (5 balancing practice Q) and CFU 7.2-C (mole ratio/molar mass calculation) deliver HS-PS1-7, but neither survives to the test. Add at least one balancing Q when revising the Chapter 7 test.

3. **CFU 7.4-C bolt-on is the chapter's highest-synthesis item.** The proposed bolt-on for 7.4-C (catalase + H₂O₂ → classify as decomposition, balance the equation, explain catalyst role) requires students to apply 7.2 balancing, 7.3 reaction classification, AND 7.4 enzyme/catalyst concepts in one response. It is the first CFU in the PS sequence to require cross-section synthesis. Adding it turns a 6-Q vocabulary recall CFU into the chapter's capstone assessment item.

4. **Explosions article is ready to use with zero prep.** `The Biggest Non-Nuclear Explosions in History.docx` is already in the folder and directly illustrates 7.1's exothermic reaction concept at the macro scale. Pair with the CER prompt from the table above. Best assigned after 7.1-B (after students can distinguish exothermic from endothermic).

5. **`Copy of Chapter 7.4 - A - Notes.pptx` should be renamed.** This 7-slide deck is the only 7.4-A sub-deck; the "Copy of" prefix is misleading. Rename to `Chapter 7.4 - A - Notes.pptx` to match the naming pattern of every other sub-deck in the chapter.

6. **`CFU - 7.3 - C - subsumed in previous_.docx` should be moved.** The filename documents the decision: this CFU's content was absorbed into 7.3-B. It is not a working worksheet. Move to Clutter.

7. **`Parent Permission Form - State Science Olympia.docx` is misfiled.** Not curriculum content. Move to admin folder.

8. **[Priority CER note — Phase 3] HS-PS1-7 synthesis pair**: CFU 7.2-B (balancing practice) + the 7.2-A bolt-on (balance Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃ and explain conservation of mass) form the chapter's HS-PS1-7 application set. The bolt-on is the SEP-6 anchor; 7.2-B is the procedural practice. Together they cover the PE: mathematical representation (7.2-B) + conceptual explanation of what the math means (7.2-A bolt-on).
