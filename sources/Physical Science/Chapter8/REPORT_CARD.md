**Lab/activity column** cross-references [INVENTORY.md](../../../INVENTORY.md) to flag equipment gaps.
**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 8 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Solutions and Other Mixtures") covers three sections: 8.1 Solutions and Other Mixtures (matter classification: pure substances vs. mixtures; heterogeneous types — suspensions settle/filter, colloids smaller particles scatter light and pass through most filters, emulsions = liquid-in-liquid colloid with emulsifier; homogeneous types — solutions, alloys = solid solutions of metals; solute = substance that dissolves; solvent = substance it dissolves into), 8.2 How Substances Dissolve (water as universal solvent; **"like dissolves like"** rule — polar solvents dissolve polar and ionic solutes; water's polar character pulls apart ionic compounds; nonpolar substances resist water; energy and particle motion drive dissolution; three rate factors: surface area, temperature, stirring; colligative properties: solutes lower freezing point and raise boiling point of solvent), and 8.3 Solubility and Concentration (solubility = maximum grams of solute per 100 g solvent at given temperature; unsaturated/saturated/supersaturated; concentration = amount of solute per amount of solution; molarity = moles of solute per liter of solution; solubility of solids increases with temperature; solubility of gases decreases with temperature and increases with pressure — soda/CO₂ example). **The chapter's primary PE is HS-PS1-3**: its clarification statement explicitly calls out solubility as an example macroscopic property to explain via intermolecular electrical forces ("polar/ionic → water-soluble; nonpolar → not"). Section 8.2 is the HS-PS1-3 delivery core. The PE also calls for investigation (SEP-3 — "plan and conduct an investigation") but no lab is present in the folder; this is the chapter's most actionable gap.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 8 delivers **HS-PS1-3** — the electrical-forces-explain-solubility standard — cleanly through Section 8.2. The chapter is logically organized: 8.1 builds the vocabulary (mixture types, solute/solvent), 8.2 delivers the PE (polarity → like-dissolves-like → solubility patterns; rate factors as CCC Cause and Effect), and 8.3 extends to quantitative concepts (solubility, concentration, molarity). The IWS system is complete (8.1/8.2/8.3 all present). The sub-deck system is complete for 8.1 and 8.2 but **8.3 has no sub-deck** — only the canonical deck covers sections 24–29. The CFU set is complete per-section (8.1-A, 8.1-B, 8.2-A, 8.2-B, 8.3-A). The test (23 Q) covers the chapter well with no content errors flagged.

**Three structural gaps:** (1) **No lab** — HS-PS1-3 is an investigation-based PE but the chapter has no hands-on component; a "like-dissolves-like" polar/nonpolar investigation is natural and likely requires only existing inventory (test tubes, water, rubbing alcohol, salt, food coloring, oil); (2) **Molarity is introduced but never practiced** — defined on Sl25, recalled on CFU 8.3-A Q2, and identified by name on the test, but no calculation is worked anywhere in the chapter; if stoichiometric/concentration calculations are a course goal, a worked example and one CFU calculation Q are missing; (3) **Compressibility**: Ch8 is the most compressible chapter in the PS sequence — 8.1's colloid/emulsion/suspension vocabulary distinctions are important context but have no PE weight; if a physics insertion window is needed, 8.1 is the most reducible section (1 period instead of 2–3 would lose no HS-PS1-3 content).

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **No lab for an investigation-based PE — highest-priority gap.** HS-PS1-3's clarification statement calls for comparing structure to infer electrical force effects on solubility. A minimal "like-dissolves-like" lab: students test NaCl (ionic/polar), food coloring (polar), and oil (nonpolar) in water (polar) and rubbing alcohol or mineral oil (nonpolar) — observing which combinations dissolve. Test tubes and the listed solutes are almost certainly in inventory; rubbing alcohol (isopropanol) likely in §3 solvents. This lab directly earns the SEP-3 investigation dimension the PE requires and requires zero specialized equipment.

2. **Molarity gap — introduced but never practiced.** Sl25 defines molarity; CFU 8.3-A Q2 asks "what is molarity?"; the test Q8 asks what unit measures concentration. At no point does a student compute molarity (moles/liters). If this is a course expectation, add one worked example in the canonical deck after Sl25 and one calculation Q to CFU 8.3-A. If not a calculation expectation, the current depth is appropriate.

3. **`CFU - 9.1 - B.docx` misfiled — move to Chapter 9.** The filename and content (strong acid, electrolyte, acid safety PPE) are clearly Ch9 material. The "Solutions and Other Mixtures" header appears to have been copied from a Ch8 template when this CFU was created. Move to `sources/Physical Science/Chapter9/`.

4. **`Copy of CFU - 8.2 - A.docx` is misnamed — rename or remove.** The file is titled "Worksheet 8.2 - A" but contains 8.2-B questions (energy, surface area, temperature, stirring). A teacher looking for the like-dissolves-like CFU gets the wrong content. Either rename to `CFU - 8.2 - B - v2.docx` (it is a functional second version of 8.2-B at distinct md5) or move to Clutter.

5. **8.3 has no sub-deck.** If the section sub-deck pacing system is in use, 8.3 (solubility/concentration/molarity/pressure, Sl24-29 of canonical) has no standalone deck. For consistency with 8.1-A/B and 8.2-A/B, a `Chapter 8.3 - A Notes.pptx` (definitions: solubility, saturation, supersaturation, concentration, molarity) would close the pacing gap.

6. **Priority CER bolt-on: CFU 8.2-A.** The like-dissolves-like prediction item proposed above (NaCl ionic + oil nonpolar; water polar + hexane nonpolar; predict which dissolves in which) is the chapter's HS-PS1-3 application item. It requires applying molecular structure → electrical force → macroscopic solubility in a novel case — the exact reasoning chain HS-PS1-3 targets.

7. **Compressibility assessment (physics insertion planning).** Ch8 is the stronger candidate for compression over Ch9 (Acids/Bases). Section 8.1 (heterogeneous mixture taxonomy) is PE-free vocabulary. A compressed Ch8 that preserves only 8.2 (like-dissolves-like) and the core of 8.3 (saturated/unsaturated/molarity as a concept) could be delivered in 4–5 class periods vs. the current 7–8, freeing time for a PS2 forces/motion insert if desired.
