# REPORT_CARD — Physical Science Chapter 1: The Nature of Science (Introduction to Science)

**Audit date:** 2026-06-11
**Standards:** ND 2019 Science Content Standards
**Scope:** Canonical full-chapter deck + per-section breakout decks, CFU worksheets, skills worksheets (scientific notation, SI conversion, graphing), the Measurements Lab and its publisher datasheets, the lab-equipment lesson/maze, Int-Readers (the IWS equivalent for this course), and the AI-generated chapter test — publisher `chapter_test_a/b/final` docx tests, both `.tst` files, the ExamView PDFs, and the three `Physical Science - Chapter 1 - Review.xlsx` review-game exports excluded
**Lab/activity column** cross-references the room's [INVENTORY.md](../../../INVENTORY.md) (§ = inventory section) to flag where a hands-on component exists or could plug in, and whether the equipment is on hand.
**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 1 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Introduction to Science") is the **skills foundation for the whole course**, in three sections: 1.1 The Nature of Science (science vs. technology, social vs. natural science, law / theory / model), 1.2 The Way Science Works (scientific methods, critical thinking, controlled experiments, bias, SI units and prefixes), 1.3 Organizing Data (scientific notation, tables, line graphs, independent/dependent variables). **It owns no Performance Expectation** — in the ND 2019 framework this content lives inside the Science & Engineering Practices that every later PE assumes: *planning and carrying out investigations* (1.2 + the Measurements Lab), *using mathematics and computational thinking* (1.3 scientific notation, SI conversion), and *analyzing and interpreting data* (1.3 graphing). The right question for this chapter is not "which PE does it hit" but "does it build the practices the PS1/PS3 chapters will lean on" — and mostly, yes.

---

**Chapter verdict:** The strongest *practice* chapter audited so far in either course. Where Biology's pattern was "recall CFUs, equipped-but-missing labs," PS Chapter 1 inverts it: the Measurements Lab is present, differentiated at three levels, and every piece of equipment is in the room; and section 1.3's assessment is genuine applied skill work (notation conversion, graph construction) rather than definition recall. The weaknesses are the familiar ones in miniature — the 1.1/1.2 CFUs are pure recall, and the version clutter is the heaviest yet (now reduced: five byte-identical files removed this audit). One real content repair: test Q18's "liters = SI base unit" error.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **The Measurements Lab is ready to run — schedule it, don't rebuild it.** Custom datasheet plus publisher A/B/C tiers plus an area inquiry lab, with 100% of the equipment in INVENTORY §2/§3. The only decision is whether to restore the mass/balance station the custom version trimmed (datasheets B/C still have it; balances are on hand). This is the Phase-2 counterexample: nothing external needed here.

2. **Test repairs applied (2026-06-11, at teacher direction).** The "SI base unit for volume = liters" item was reworded to the deck's framing ("Which unit does the metric system use to measure volume?"), and the precision item (old Q13) was dropped as untaught content — test renumbered to 19 items. A third error surfaced during the edit and was fixed too: the Q12 scientific-notation key said d (4.5 × 10⁻⁴) but 0.0045 is c (4.5 × 10⁻³). Worth a skim of the other AI-generated tests in later chapters for similar key slips.

3. **CFU bolt-ons for 1.1/1.2 (the recurring CER recommendation):** the "just a theory" explanation item for 1.1-B and the design-a-controlled-experiment item for 1.2-A. Both are [short-answer] in Forms mode; both use only chapter content. Section 1.3 needs nothing — its worksheets already are the applied practice.

4. **No PE is owed by this chapter, so no standards gap.** It builds the three SEPs (investigations, math thinking, data analysis) that the PS1 chemistry chapters and the PS3 energy material will assume. The course-level gap to keep tracking — forces (PS2-2/2-3) and energy (PS3-1/2/3) vs an all-chemistry chapter set — is unaffected by Ch1 either way.
