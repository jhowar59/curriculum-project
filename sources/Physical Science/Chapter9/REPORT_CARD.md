Chapter 9 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Acids, Bases, and pH") covers a single section: 9.1 Acids and Bases. Content: **acids** — defined as compounds that form hydronium ions (H₃O⁺) when dissolved in water; properties (sour taste, conduct electricity, turn blue litmus red, corrosive); **strong acids** ionize completely when dissolved (HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃ — all strong electrolytes); **weak acids** ionize partially (acetic acid, formic acid, sulfurous acid — weak electrolytes); electrolyte = any substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water; **bases** — defined as compounds that form hydroxide ions (OH⁻) when dissolved in water; properties (bitter taste, slippery feel, conduct electricity, turn red litmus blue, corrosive); **strong bases** ionize completely — most are metal hydroxides (NaOH/lye); **weak bases** ionize partially (ammonia); safety reminders for both classes; **indicators** — substances that change color in acidic or basic solutions (litmus paper, pH paper); **pH scale** — 0–14, with <7 = acid, >7 = base, 7 = neutral; measured with a pH meter. The deck paces with two "Stop" markers (Sl7 and Sl15) splitting acids from bases/indicators. No quantitative content is included — no calculations, no pH conversions, no neutralization reactions. **PE mapping for the PS course:** of the PS-course-owned PEs, **HS-PS1-5** has the closest connection — strong/weak acids differ in their degree of ionization (concentration of H₃O⁺ released), which affects reactivity and conductivity. The chapter doesn't frame this in terms of reaction rates, but the ionization-degree concept is the accessible entry point for PS1-5 if extended. **HS-PS1-2** is a weaker but real hook: acid-base reactions involve ionic charge transfer that follows directly from the bonding and periodic table work of Ch4–6. No single PE is the unambiguous primary delivery target here — this is the only PS chapter without a clear PE anchor.

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 9 is **the thinnest chapter in the PS set** by every measure: one section, one deck (15 slides), two recall CFUs, no IWS, no lab, no test, and an empty placeholder doc. The content — acids/bases definitions, strong/weak distinction, indicators, pH scale — is clean foundational chemistry vocabulary and is internally consistent across the deck and CFUs. The chapter delivers no PE as its primary anchor; HS-PS1-5 (ionization degree → reactivity/conductivity) is the closest mapping but is never stated in those terms. The strong/weak distinction is presented as a vocabulary fact rather than a scientific principle to apply.

**Three structural gaps:** (1) **No test** — the first PS chapter with no assessment at all (`Chapter 9.docx` is a blank shell); (2) **No lab** — a household pH survey is the most accessible lab opportunity in the entire PS course (supplies: pH paper + kitchen items) and would make the pH scale concept concrete; (3) **No PE anchor** — the chapter would benefit from a brief framing that connects ionization degree to reaction rate (HS-PS1-5) or to the ionic bonding concepts of Ch6/Ch8 (HS-PS1-2), which would lift it from vocabulary review to applied reasoning.

**Compressibility:** This is the most compressible chapter in the PS course — 15 slides, 2 CFUs, 1 section, ~2–3 class periods. If physics insertion time is needed (PS2-2/2-3 forces/momentum or PS3 energy — both confirmed gaps across all 9 chemistry chapters), Ch9 is the first candidate for compression, ahead of Ch8 8.1.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **No test — first PS chapter with no assessment.** `Chapter 9.docx` contains only a blank title header. No ExamView file, no AI-generated test, no quiz. If the chapter is taught, a short 10–15 Q test is needed. Given the vocabulary-heavy content, MCQ is appropriate. The Ch1 pattern (AI-generated MCQ reviewed and corrected) would work here; flag for Phase 3 if no OER test is found in Phase 2.

2. **No lab for a chapter built around observable properties.** Every property listed on the slides (sour taste, litmus color, conductivity, slippery feel) is directly observable. A household pH survey — students test vinegar (pH ~2.5), lemon juice (~2), milk (~6.5), tap water (~7), baking soda solution (~8.5), soap (~9–10), ammonia (~11) with pH paper — costs nothing beyond pH paper and requires no lab period setup. This also provides the data set for the priority CER bolt-on below. Equipment gap: **pH paper** (confirm or add to SUPPLIES_NEEDED.md — not listed in INVENTORY).

3. **Priority CER bolt-on: CFU 9.1-B Q2.** Current Q2 = "What is the pH of a solution?" (definition recall). Bolt-on: *"A student tests four liquids with pH paper: vinegar (pH 3), milk (pH 6.5), distilled water (pH 7), and baking soda solution (pH 9). Rank them from most acidic to most basic. Then predict: if she mixes equal amounts of the vinegar and baking soda solution, will the mixture be acidic, basic, or neutral? Explain your reasoning using the pH scale and the definitions of acids and bases."* Applies pH scale + acid-base definitions + prediction — the first reasoning item in the chapter. Pairs naturally with the household pH lab if taught.

4. **Leading space in ` CFU - 9.1 - A.docx` filename — rename.** Same hazard as Ch8's ` CFU - 8.1 - A - 2.docx`: invisible in most file explorers, breaks command-line operations. Rename to `CFU - 9.1 - A.docx`.

5. **Ch8 misfiled 9.1-B copy — delete, don't move.** The Ch8 report card recommended moving `CFU - 9.1 - B.docx` from Ch8 to Ch9. However: the Ch8 copy (md5: d8816a636) contains acid/electrolyte questions (strong acid, electrolyte, safety PPE) — the same content as Ch9's 9.1-A — not bases/pH content. Ch9 already has a correct 9.1-B (bases/pH) at a different md5. Moving the Ch8 copy to Ch9 would create a misnamed near-duplicate of 9.1-A. **Correct action: delete the Ch8 copy** (it was likely created by copying the 9.1-A template and forgetting to update the filename and header).

6. **Physics gap wrap-up — confirmed across all 9 chapters.** This final chapter completes the PS audit. Confirmed: **no chapter addresses PS2-2/2-3 (momentum, Newton's laws) or PS3-3 (heat transfer)**. PS3-1/2 (kinetic theory/energy) landed in Ch3.
