**Lab/activity column** cross-references the room's [INVENTORY.md](../../../INVENTORY.md) (§ = inventory section) to flag where a hands-on component exists or could plug in, and whether the equipment is on hand.
**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 2 (Holt Science Spectrum, "Matter") is the **conceptual foundation of the chemistry strand**: 2.1 Classifying Matter (elements/compounds/mixtures, atoms, chemical formulas, homo/heterogeneous mixtures, miscible/immiscible), 2.2 Properties of Matter (physical properties — color/volume/mass/density/melting pt/boiling pt, intensive vs. extensive, the D=m/V equation with worked example; chemical properties — flammability, nonflammability, reactivity, steel oxidation), 2.3 Changes of Matter (physical changes, dissolving, chemical changes and their signs, separating mixtures by distillation/filtration/evaporation/magnet, separating compounds by electrolysis). The chapter maps most directly to **HS-PS1-3** (plan and conduct an investigation gathering evidence of characteristic properties that allow identification of substances) and — via the endo/exo lab — pre-figures **HS-PS3-1/PS3-2** (energy changes in chemical reactions, which this course owns).

**Chapter verdict:** Chapter 2 has the deepest lab footprint of any chapter audited so far, and one of those labs is an exact PS3-1/PS3-2 match. The endo/exo lab (2026 version) is **ready to run** — all consumables confirmed. The CFU 2.2 A already has a worked density calculation built in (unusual and good). The one equipment uncertainty is the metals lab, which calls for solid metal test-piece sets and a hot plate that aren't confirmed in inventory. Standards coverage is solid: PS1-3 is the core PE, with the endo/exo lab pushing ahead into PS3-1/3-2 (the course's energy strand).

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **The endo/exo lab (2026 version) is ready — schedule it.** All consumables in §5 (baking soda, CaCl₂), all equipment in §2/§3 (balance, thermometer, graduated cylinder, beakers). The 2026 version adds quantitative precision (grams, mL) over the older tablespoon version. This is the Ch2 lab to run and it directly covers the PS3-1/PS3-2 energy PEs.

2. **Check storage for metal test-piece sets and a hot plate before planning the metals lab.** The publisher physical-properties lab is an excellent HS-PS1-3 investigation (density + hardness + conductivity + magnetism, with an engineering-design wrapper), but it requires solid aluminum/iron/nickel/tin/zinc pieces and a hot plate for the conductivity test — neither confirmed in INVENTORY. If the pieces aren't in a cabinet somewhere, this lab needs a one-time purchase (metal sample sets run ~$15–30). The hot plate is a common item that may simply be unlisted.

3. **CFU 2.3 A is the chapter's priority bolt-on.** The endo/exo lab is the hands-on hook; a paired [short-answer] item — "two signs of chemical change in the baking soda + vinegar reaction, and what they tell you about what's happening" — closes the argument from evidence. The 2.2B chemical-property item and 2.1A formula-interpretation item are lower priority but worth adding when the Forms-mode CFUs are built.

4. **No CFU 2.3 B, and that's fine.** Section 2.3 is covered by a single 6-slide segment with no A/B split; one CFU is proportionate.

5. **Review worksheet and pretest are near-identical (Q1–7 overlap).** Pick one for class use; circulating both will cause confusion at no benefit.
