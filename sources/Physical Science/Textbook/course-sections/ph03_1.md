#### **Potential Energy**

When you stretch a rubber band, you do work. The energy used to stretch the rubber band is stored until you release the rubber band. When you release the rubber band, it flies from your hand. But where is the energy between the time you stretch the rubber band and the time you release the rubber band?

A stretched rubber band stores energy in a form called **potential energy.** Potential energy (*PE*) is sometimes called *energy of position* because it results from the relative **positions of objects in a system.** Any object that is stretched or compressed to increase or decrease the distance between its parts has potential energy that is called *elastic potential energy*. Stretched bungee cords and compressed springs have elastic potential energy.

Imagine that you are at the top of the first hill of the roller coaster shown in **Figure 2.** Your position above the ground gives you energy that could potentially do work as you move toward the ground. Any system of two or more objects separated by a vertical distance has potential energy that results from the gravitational attraction between the objects. This kind of stored energy is called *gravitational potential energy*.
