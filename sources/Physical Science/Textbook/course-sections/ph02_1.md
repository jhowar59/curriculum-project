Every change in motion that you observe or feel is caused by a force. Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727), a British scientist, described the relationship between motion and force in three laws that we now call *Newton's laws of motion*. Newton's laws apply to a wide range of motion—a caterpillar crawling on a leaf, a person riding a bicycle, or a rocket moving in space.

If you start your book sliding across a rough surface, such as carpet, the book soon comes to rest. On a smooth surface, such as ice, the book will slide much farther. Because there is less frictional force between the ice and the book, the smaller force must act over a longer time before the book stops. Without friction, the book would keep sliding.
