## 1 — Synopsis (teacher one-pager)

**What this section covers:**
§6.1 establishes the language and framework for all of Chapter 6. Three main concepts:

1. **Chemical Bonds** — a compound is two or more elements chemically combined; chemical bonds are the attractive forces holding atoms or ions together. The H₂ + O₂ → H₂O reaction illustrates bond-breaking and bond-forming.
2. **Chemical Structure** — the arrangement of atoms in a compound. Three model types are introduced: *ball-and-stick* (shows bond angles and relative geometry), *space-filling* (shows relative atomic sizes), and *structural formula* (uses chemical symbols and lines). Two geometry terms: *bond length* (average distance between bonded nuclei) and *bond angle* (angle formed by two bonds to the same atom). Bonds are flexible, not rigid like sticks — they can bend, stretch, and rotate.
3. **How Does Structure Affect Properties?** — the section's key concept: **chemical structure determines the properties of a compound**. Network solids (quartz, SiO₂) are hard and have very high melting/boiling points because their bonds are extensive and strong. Ionic networks (NaCl) are also strong solids. Molecular compounds (water, sugar) have lower melting points.

## 3 — Daily open-notes CER (graded today, open own notes)

> **Prompt:**
>
> A geologist picks up two rock samples. Sample A is quartz (silicon dioxide, SiO₂) — it scratches glass and has a melting point of 1,700 °C. Sample B is a sugar crystal — it dissolves easily in water and melts at about 186 °C. Both substances are solid at room temperature and look superficially similar.
>
> **Using your notes from today**, explain why Sample A is so much harder and harder to melt than Sample B. Your explanation must include the words *chemical structure*, *bond*, and at least one of the following: *network*, *molecule*, or *bond angle/bond length*. Write 3–5 sentences.
>
> *(Open own notes; graded on: claim stated, explanation references structure → property chain, at least two vocab terms used correctly)*

**Scoring note (for Forms [short-answer] mode):** Brisk grades on claim + structure-referenced reasoning + two vocab terms. Key answer: quartz has a network structure of strong covalent Si–O–Si bonds extending throughout the solid; it takes enormous energy to break them, so the melting point is extremely high. Sugar is made of separate molecules held by weaker forces; less energy needed to separate them.

**Why this prompt works:** Chunk C delivers the key concept; the CER immediately asks students to apply it to a real, concrete comparison. Good notes (Chunk C stop-question answered) = answerable in 5 minutes. Thin notes = cannot identify the structure-type difference. This is also the conceptual foundation for every CER in §§6.2–6.4.
