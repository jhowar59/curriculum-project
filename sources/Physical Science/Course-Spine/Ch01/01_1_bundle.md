## 1 — Synopsis (teacher one-pager)

**What this section covers:**
§1.1 introduces *what science is* and *how scientists work*, using Wilhelm Roentgen's 1895 X-ray discovery as the narrative spine. Three main concepts:

1. **How Science Takes Place** — the scientific method as a cycle: question → plan → observe → confirm. Roentgen's story illustrates all four steps in a memorable, non-lab-manual way.
2. **The Branches of Science** — biological, physical, and Earth science as the three main divisions; interdisciplinary branches (biochemistry, geophysics) as a preview of cross-cutting connections. Pure science vs. technology.
3. **Models in Science** — what a scientific model is; physical, mathematical, and computer models. Bridges to later chapters where atomic/molecular models will appear.

**OCR gap:** The "Scientific Laws and Theories" sub-topic (listed in the chapter outline) did not survive the OCR cleanly in this section file. If the textbook is available for in-class reading, this content will surface naturally; if not, it can be covered briefly in teacher talk (≈2 min): "A **law** describes *what* happens; a **theory** explains *why* it happens. Both are supported by repeated evidence."

**Chunk breakdown (3 reading chunks, ~5–8 min each):**
- **Chunk A:** "How Science Takes Place" → Roentgen story (pp. 5-9 approx). Stop point: after "Scientists always confirm results." Cue: *What does Roentgen do when he gets an unexpected result?*
- **Chunk B:** "The Branches of Science" + Technology. Stop point: after the technology/pure science distinction. Cue: *What branch of science is chemistry? What branch is physics?*
- **Chunk C:** "Models in Science." Stop point: end of section. Cue: *What is a scientific model? Give one example from the text.*

**Day 1 goal:** Establish the Cornell format. Model the think-aloud on Chunk A; students do Chunks B and C more independently. The open-notes CER closes the period.

## 2 — Cue-column questions (Cornell left column)

*These seed the left column of the Cornell sheet. Students write the Q before reading; add answer during the stop; summarize at the bottom.*

### Chunk A — How Science Takes Place
1. What four steps describe how scientists explore the world?
2. Why did Roentgen cover the tube with black paper?
3. What did Roentgen do when he got an unexpected result — and why?

### Chunk B — Branches of Science + Technology
4. What are the three main branches of natural science?
5. What is the difference between *pure science* and *technology*?
6. Give one example of a branch of science that crosses two main branches.

### Chunk C — Models
7. What is a scientific model?
8. Name three *types* of scientific models.

### (Teacher add-on — laws vs. theories, if not in textbook section)
9. What is the difference between a scientific **law** and a scientific **theory**?

## 3 — Daily open-notes CER (graded today, open own notes)

> **Prompt:**
>
> A student reads that Roentgen "covered the glass tube with heavy black paper" to check his equipment, but then saw a glow from a detector almost 1 m away — which surprised him. Using your notes, **explain** why Roentgen did NOT ignore this unexpected result and instead ran more experiments. What does his response show about how science works? Use at least one vocab term from the Key Terms list (science, technology, law, theory) in your answer.
>
> *(3–5 sentences; open own notes; graded on: claim stated, reasoning from evidence, at least one term used correctly)*

**Scoring note (for Forms [short-answer] mode):** Brisk can grade on the 3-part rubric above. Paper version: scan for claim + Roentgen + at least one term; quick holistic grade.

**Why this prompt works as the first CER of the year:** It uses Chunk A content (which students read that period) + a vocab term + a SEP-level reasoning ask ("what does his response show about how science works"). Thin notes = can't answer part 2. Good notes = cash in immediately.
