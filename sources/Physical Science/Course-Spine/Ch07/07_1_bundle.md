Three main concept groups:

1. **What is a Chemical Reaction?** — Chemical reactions occur when substances undergo chemical changes to form new substances. Observable clues: color change, gas released, heat or light given off, solid (precipitate) forms. At the atomic level: bonds break and atoms rearrange to form new bonds. Key vocabulary: *reactants* (substances going in) and *products* (substances formed).
2. **Chemical Energy** — Energy is stored in chemical bonds. *Chemical energy* = energy stored in the bonds of substances. Photosynthesis is the textbook's primary example: light energy is converted into chemical energy stored in carbohydrate bonds.
3. **Endothermic vs. Exothermic Reactions** — All reactions involve energy change. *Exothermic* reactions release more energy than they absorb; products have **less** energy than reactants; the surroundings warm up. *Endothermic* reactions absorb more energy than they release; products have **MORE** energy than reactants; the surroundings cool down. Law of Conservation of Energy applies: energy is not created or destroyed, only transferred or transformed.

## 3 — Daily open-notes CER (graded today, open own notes)

> **Prompt:**
>
> A hand warmer gets hot when activated. A cold pack gets cold when squeezed and activated.
>
> Using your notes from today, **explain** which reaction is exothermic and which is endothermic. For each one: (a) state whether the products have more or less energy than the reactants, and (b) explain why the surroundings change temperature in the direction they do. Use the terms *exothermic*, *endothermic*, *chemical energy*, *reactants*, and *products* in your answer.
>
> *(3–5 sentences; open own notes; graded on: both objects correctly identified, energy direction stated for each, temperature change correctly explained)*

**Scoring note:** Hand warmer = exothermic (releases energy → surroundings warm up → products have less energy than reactants). Cold pack = endothermic (absorbs energy from surroundings → surroundings cool → products have more energy than reactants). Students who mixed up the definitions in their notes will get at least one wrong. In Forms [short-answer] mode, Brisk can grade on the 3-part rubric.

**Why this prompt works:** It applies the Chunk C endothermic/exothermic distinction to two familiar consumer objects — a classic paired-contrast format that forces students to hold both definitions simultaneously. The five required terms are all from the section's Key Terms list, so notes quality is directly tested.
