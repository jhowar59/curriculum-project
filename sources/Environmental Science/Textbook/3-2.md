The Movement of Energy in the Atmosphere Air that is constantly moving upward, downward, or sideways causes Earth's weather. In the troposphere, currents of less dense air, warmed by the Earth's surface, rise into the atmosphere and currents of denser cold air sink toward the ground. As a current of air rises into the atmosphere, it begins to cool. Eventually, the air current becomes more dense than the air around it and sinks instead of continuing to rise. So, the air current moves back toward Earth's surface until it is heated by the Earth and becomes less dense. Then, the air current begins to rise again.
