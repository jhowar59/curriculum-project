However, the green revolution also had some negative effects.

Most new varieties of grain produce large yields only if they receive large amounts of water, fertilizer, and pesticides. In addition, the machinery, irrigation, and chemicals required by new crop varieties can degrade the soil if they are not used properly. As a result of the overuse of fertilizers and pesticides, yields from green revolution crops are falling in many areas. Before the green revolution, subsistence farmers worked most of the world's farmland. But they could not afford the equipment, water, and chemicals needed to grow the new crop varieties.
