## ND Standards Owned by This Course

9 Performance Expectations, identified by "Environmental Science" course label in
the ND clarification statements:

| Code | Topic |
|------|-------|
| HS-ESS2-2 | Analyze geoscience data to make the claim that one change to Earth's surface can create feedbacks that cause changes to other Earth systems (climate feedbacks, glacier/ice loss, runoff, wetlands) |
| HS-ESS2-5 | Plan and conduct an investigation of the properties of water and its effects on Earth materials and surface processes (hydrologic cycle, rock cycle) |
| HS-ESS2-6 | Develop a quantitative model to describe the cycling of carbon among the hydrosphere, atmosphere, geosphere, and biosphere |
| HS-ESS3-1 | Construct an explanation for how the availability of natural resources, natural hazards, and changes in climate have influenced human activity |
| HS-ESS3-2 | Evaluate competing design solutions for developing, managing, and utilizing energy and mineral resources based on cost-benefit ratios |
| HS-ESS3-3 | Analyze the relationships among management of natural resources, the sustainability of human populations, and biodiversity |
| HS-ESS3-4 | Evaluate or refine a technological solution that reduces impacts of human activities on natural systems |
| HS-ESS3-5 | Analyze geoscience data and global climate models to make an evidence-based forecast of climate change and associated future impacts |
| HS-ESS3-6 | Use data from computational representations to illustrate the relationships among Earth systems and how those relationships are being modified due to human activity |

**NOT this course's:** all ESS1 (stars/universe/Earth formation) + ESS2-1, 2-3,

## Daily Instructional Rhythm

**One textbook section = one instructional day.** Sections average 6–9 pages of
student-facing prose. The course is reading-driven with light lecture framing —
lecture is the 10-minute hook, not the main event.

| Phase | ~Time | What |
|-------|-------|------|
| Framing | 10 min | Mini-lecture, case-study hook, or Maps-in-Action warm-up sets the section's driving question |
| Reading | 20 min | Students read the section (in class or pre-assigned) |
| Check | 15 min | CFU question set on the section — QuestionWell export (paper / Forms / Kahoot depending on the day) |
| Discuss | ~10 min | Case study tie-in, current event, or ND-local connection |

Chapters are 2–3 sections → each chapter is a **2–3 day arc**, followed by a
project window or chapter assessment.
