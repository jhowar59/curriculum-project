> **[Teaching Tip]**
>
> Many parents measure their child's height every year by marking notches on a door post. Point out that in this situation, the child's growth each year is the displacement. The child's growth rate (velocity) is that displacement divided by the time (1 year), and the change in growth rate per unit of time (acceleration) is the difference in the growth rate from one year to the next.
