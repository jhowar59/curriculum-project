**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 4 covers chemical energy & ATP (4.1), overview of photosynthesis (4.2), photosynthesis in detail (4.3), overview of cellular respiration (4.4), respiration in detail (4.5), and fermentation (4.6). **This is the chapter where the HS-LS energy standards live** — HS-LS1-5 (photosynthesis), HS-LS1-7 (respiration), and HS-LS2-3 (energy flow in aerobic vs. anaerobic conditions).

**Chapter verdict:** 11 of 13 in-scope files leave-be on content. Chapter 4 is conceptually the **energy heart of the course** — it carries three process standards (HS-LS1-5, HS-LS1-7, HS-LS2-3) — but the materials deliver it entirely as lecture + recall. The two things worth acting on are a striking lab gap and one high-value CER.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **Lab gap — and the room is the best-equipped yet (headline).** There is **no lab worksheet anywhere in Chapter 4**, yet this is the chapter the inventory most directly supports:
   - **Photosynthesis (4.2–4.3, HS-LS1-5):** the **Carolina Plant Pigments & Photosynthesis Kit, 8-station, is on hand (INVENTORY §7)** — leaf-pigment chromatography straight out of the box. A leaf-disk floating assay is also fully resourced: syringes (§4), beakers (§3), baking soda (§5), bench lamps (§1/§6).
   - **Respiration / fermentation (4.5–4.6, HS-LS1-7, HS-LS2-3):** a yeast-fermentation investigation needs only sugar (§5), test tubes/flasks (§3), and thermometers (§2) — all in stock. This one doubles as the hands-on evidence for the 4.6 CER.
   Building even one of these turns the chapter's process standards into "plan/conduct an investigation" or "use a model" experiences instead of reading.

2. **CER bolt-on → HS-LS2-3, the aerobic-vs-anaerobic explanation.** This is the single highest-value revision in the chapter. CFU 4.6 is fill-in-blank recall about fermentation; HS-LS2-3 explicitly asks students to *construct and revise an explanation for the cycling of matter and flow of energy in aerobic and anaerobic conditions*. One **[short-answer]** item on CFU 4.6 (compare ATP yield and conditions for aerobic respiration vs. fermentation) hits it. The deck's photosynthesis↔respiration "mirror image" framing extends this into a full energy-and-matter synthesis if you want a larger CER.

3. **CFU coverage gap.** CFUs exist for 4.2, 4.3, 4.5, 4.6 — **none for 4.1 (Chemical Energy & ATP) or 4.4 (Overview of Cellular Respiration)**. 4.4 is the better one to add, since it's the natural home for the HS-LS2-3 synthesis item.

4. **Digitization is already underway.** CFU 4.3's header records that it was "replaced with a Google quiz ala QuestionWell." That's the model for the rest — and a reminder that for 4.3 the bolt-on lands in that existing Form's question set, not the paper doc.
