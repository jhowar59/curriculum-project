## Part A — The Carbon Cycle (HS-LS2-5)

### Slide 1 — Photosynthesis and respiration cycle carbon

**KEY CONCEPT**
The same two processes from this chapter move carbon between living things and the environment.

- **Review (4.2):** photosynthesis pulls **CO₂ out of the air** to build sugar. `6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂`
- **Review (4.4):** cellular respiration breaks sugar down and puts **CO₂ back into the air**. `C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O`
- That "mirror image" (Slide 4.4) is the **biological engine of the carbon cycle** — the same carbon atoms move between organisms and the atmosphere again and again.

### Slide 2 — Carbon moves among four Earth systems

- **Definition:** the **carbon cycle** is the movement of carbon among living things and Earth's systems.
- **Biosphere** (living things) ↔ **Atmosphere** (CO₂ gas): photosynthesis takes carbon *in*; respiration and decomposition send it *out*.
- **Hydrosphere** (oceans/water): CO₂ dissolves into and out of water.
- **Geosphere** (rock, soil, fossil fuels): carbon is stored long-term; burning fossil fuels releases it.
- **Definition:** decomposers release stored carbon back to the air as they break down dead organisms.

### Slide 3 — Model the carbon cycle *(LS2-5 SEP: develop a model)*

- Build/draw a model with arrows showing carbon's path. Label the **process** on each arrow:
  - atmosphere → biosphere (**photosynthesis**)
  - biosphere → atmosphere (**cellular respiration**, **decomposition**)
  - atmosphere ↔ hydrosphere (**dissolving in oceans**)
  - geosphere → atmosphere (**burning fossil fuels / combustion**)
- **Cause & Effect note:** humans burn fossil fuels faster than photosynthesis removes CO₂, so atmospheric carbon rises — the model shows *why*.

## Part B — Energy Flow & Pyramids (HS-LS2-4)

### Slide 4 — Energy flows one way through an ecosystem

**KEY CONCEPT**
Carbon *cycles*, but energy *flows one way* — in as sunlight, out as heat.

- **Review:** photosynthesis stores light energy as chemical energy (sugar); respiration releases it as ATP **and heat**.
- **Definition:** a **trophic level** is a feeding step in a food chain — producers → primary consumers → secondary consumers → …
- Energy enters as sunlight, is captured by producers, and is passed up the chain — but never recycled the way carbon is.

### Slide 5 — The 10% rule and the energy pyramid

- Only about **10%** of the energy at one trophic level is stored and passed to the next.
- The other ~90% is lost — mostly as **heat from cellular respiration** and used for life processes.
- **Example:** 10,000 kcal in plants → ~1,000 kcal in herbivores → ~100 kcal in carnivores.
- An **energy pyramid** (and a **biomass pyramid**) is widest at the producer level for this reason — and it's why food chains rarely run more than 4–5 levels.

### Slide 6 — Calculate with the pyramid *(LS2-4 SEP: mathematical representation)*

- Given the energy at the producer level, multiply by **0.10** at each step up to find what reaches each level.
- Practice problem is on the CFU below.
