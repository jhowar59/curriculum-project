**Lab/activity column** cross-references the room's [INVENTORY.md](../../../INVENTORY.md) (§ = inventory section) to flag where a hands-on component exists or could plug in, and whether the equipment is on hand.
**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 3 covers cell theory (3.1), eukaryotic organelles (3.2), the cell membrane (3.3), diffusion & osmosis (3.4), and active transport / endo- & exocytosis (3.5).

**Chapter verdict:** 12 of 14 in-scope files leave-be. Chapter 3 is in better shape than 1–2 on the hands-on axis — it ships **two real labs** (onion-cell microscopy and a cell-model build), both mapping to HS-LS1-2 and both fully resourced in the room. The written work is still all recall: every CFU is content-only.

**Two findings worth acting on:**

1. **CER bolt-on, transport sections (3.3–3.5 → HS-LS1-3)** — delivered as **[short-answer]** items on the new section CFUs (see #2). This is the chapter's best causal-reasoning opportunity, and the publisher already models it: IWS 3.4 Big Picture Q6 (reason through water movement in a hypertonic cell) and IWS 3.3 Q5 (explain the bilayer arrangement) are ready-made prompts for the reasoning item — whether you write the CFU on paper or generate it.

2. **CFU coverage gap.** CFUs exist only for 3.1 and 3.2. **There is no CFU for 3.3, 3.4, or 3.5** — the three sections carrying the HS-LS1-3 content and the most reasoning potential. Closing it is cheap either way — write a short paper CFU, or generate section quizzes from the 3.3–3.5 slides. If you generate, the catch is the prompt: left to default it produces three more recall-only quizzes and reproduces the gap in spirit, so it has to **explicitly request one [short-answer] application item** (use the IWS Q6/Q5 prompts above as the template). Highest-value single addition is a diffusion/osmosis (3.4) check, paired directly with the osmosis lab below.

**Lab/activity note:** The room is equipped for an even stronger transport unit than the files currently use. The **Carolina Diffusion-Osmosis BioKit is on hand (INVENTORY §7)**, and a classic egg- or potato-osmosis investigation needs only balances and glassware (§2–3) — either one turns the 3.4 content into a "plan and conduct an investigation" experience that directly satisfies HS-LS1-3, instead of leaving diffusion/osmosis as reading + lecture. The onion-cell lab (3.1–3.2) and cell-model build (3.2) are already equipped and need nothing added.
