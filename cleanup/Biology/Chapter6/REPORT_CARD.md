**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 6 runs the full HMH heredity sequence: chromosomes / diploid-haploid (6.1), the process of meiosis (6.2), Mendel & the law of segregation (6.3), traits-genes-alleles & genotype/phenotype (6.4), Punnett squares & probability (6.5), and meiosis & genetic variation — crossing over, independent assortment, genetic linkage (6.6). It maps to a tight **HS-LS3 cluster**: **HS-LS3-1** (DNA/chromosomes carry the instructions for inherited traits → 6.1, 6.4), **HS-LS3-2** (heritable variation from new combinations in meiosis → 6.2, 6.6), and **HS-LS3-3** (apply statistics/probability to the variation and distribution of traits in a population → 6.5 *and* the class trait-frequency activity).

**Chapter verdict:** The strongest chapter so far. Coverage is complete across all six sections, the standards cluster is clean (HS-LS3-1/2/3), and — unlike Chapters 1–5, where the hands-on piece was usually *missing* — **the data/activity component is already built**: the human-trait survey (decks + observation sheet + class spreadsheet + graphing sheet) is a ready-made HS-LS3-3 investigation, and three Punnett worksheets give real problem-solving practice. Delivery is still lecture + recall on the CFU side, but two sections (6.5 worksheet, the Punnett sheets) already carry genuine reasoning. The gaps are smaller and more surgical than in earlier chapters.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **The trait survey is a complete HS-LS3-3 investigation that's one step short of the standard.** Collecting class trait frequencies and graphing them is exactly "statistics and probability to explain the variation and distribution of expressed traits in a population" — but the Graphing worksheet stops at the bar chart. Add a single **[short-answer]** interpretation prompt (*"use the class data and probability to explain why the observed ratios don't match a clean 3:1"*) and the activity *is* HS-LS3-3, with **no new materials and no equipment** required. This is the highest-value, lowest-cost move in the chapter.

2. **HS-LS3-2 is half-covered — by design, but worth a deliberate call.** The chapter nails the "new genetic combinations through meiosis" prong of HS-LS3-2 (6.2 phases, 6.6 crossing over + independent assortment + linkage). It does **not** touch the other two prongs of that PE — **replication errors and environmental mutations**. That content lives in the DNA/molecular-genetics chapter (expected next door), so this isn't a defect so much as a **handoff**: the full "make and defend a claim" HS-LS3-2 task should be assembled where mutations are taught, drawing the meiosis half from here. For Chapter 6 in isolation, the meiosis-variation claim (finding #3) is the right scope.

3. **The one CER bolt-on for the assessment is the meiosis-variation claim.** The Version B test is all recall MCQ. Its single **[short-answer]** addition should be the HS-LS3-2-flavored claim: *"Explain how meiosis — crossing over and independent assortment — produces genetically unique gametes, and why that matters for a population."* Content support is fully in 6.2/6.6; CFU 6.1-B Q6 (meiosis vs. mitosis) is the existing seed.

4. **Meiosis (6.2) is the section begging for a model, and the kit is on hand.** 6.2 is taught entirely on slides, but "model the phases of meiosis I and II" is the natural HS-LS3-2 SEP and nearly free: bead/pipe-cleaner chromosomes (craft consumables §11), with the **Carolina DNA Necklace Kit** and **Molecular Model Kit** (§7) available if you want a sturdier build. There's no CFU for 6.2 — a model-and-label activity would fill that slot better than another definitional sheet.
