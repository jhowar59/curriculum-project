**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 5 covers the cell cycle (5.1), mitosis & cytokinesis (5.2), regulation of the cell cycle & cancer (5.3), asexual reproduction (5.4), and multicellular life / differentiation (5.5). The core standard is **HS-LS1-4** (use a model to illustrate the role of mitosis and differentiation in producing and maintaining complex organisms).

**Chapter verdict:** Content coverage is solid and the chapter is conceptually clean (one clear standard, HS-LS1-4, plus a strong cancer phenomenon). As with the other chapters, delivery is lecture + recall. Three things stand out.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **Mitosis lab gap — and it's equipped.** There's **no lab worksheet in Chapter 5**, but HS-LS1-4 is hands-on by design ("use a model"). Two equipped options:
   - **Onion-root-tip mitosis** under the microscope — observe and tally cells in each phase. Microscopes (§1) and prepared-slide boxes (§1) are on hand; students can also prepare root tips.
   - **Model-the-stages activity** — pipe-cleaner/bead chromosomes through prophase→telophase (craft consumables §11). This *is* the HS-LS1-4 SEP ("use a model to illustrate") and costs almost nothing.

2. **The 5.3 cancer CER is the chapter's best reasoning hook.** Cancer is a genuine phenomenon anchor, and the regulation content (checkpoints, apoptosis, growth factors, carcinogens) supports a real explanation task: *"explain how a failure in cell-cycle regulation leads to cancer."* Two on-topic AI readings already in the folder — **"Cell Regulation and Cancer"** and **"The Cell Cycle and the Origins of Cancer"** — are ready-made evidence texts for it. There is **no CFU for 5.3**, so this is a new item, best added to the cumulative quiz as its one **[short-answer]** question.

3. **CFU coverage vs. the quiz.** Paper CFUs exist for 5.1, 5.2, 5.4 — none for 5.3 or 5.5. But the **AI-generated cumulative quiz already covers 5.3 and 5.5 in recall MCQ form**, so the *recall* gap is effectively closed digitally; what's missing is a *reasoning* item, which is exactly the 5.3 CER in #2. This is a good illustration of the two-mode workflow: the digital quiz quietly backfilled the sections the paper sheets skipped.
