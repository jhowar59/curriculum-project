**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 8 is the molecular-genetics core: identifying DNA as the genetic material via the Griffith → Avery → Hershey-Chase experiments (8.1), the structure of DNA — nucleotides, base pairing, the Franklin/Wilkins and Watson/Crick story (8.2), DNA replication (8.3), transcription and the central dogma (8.4), and translation (8.5). The whole chapter builds toward **HS-LS1-1** (*construct an explanation, based on evidence, for how the structure of DNA determines the structure of proteins that carry out life's functions*), with a strong **HS-LS3-1** link (DNA as the carrier of inherited instructions) and a genuine **history-of-science / SEP** thread in 8.1.

---

**Chapter verdict:** Content is strong and the standard (HS-LS1-1) is unusually well-served — 8.1 is a ready-made history-of-science argument, and the structure/replication sections are tailor-made for modeling with kits already on the shelf. Two things stand out, one a gap and one a mislabel. As elsewhere, delivery is lecture + recall, but the CFUs here already lean slightly more applied (the base-pairing items in 8.2/8.3).

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **Translation (8.5) is taught only by the publisher reading and the test — there's no deck and no CFU for it.** The slide decks (canonical and per-section) stop at transcription (8.4), yet `IWS 8.5.pdf` covers translation and the MCQ test includes it (codons, ribosomes, tRNA/rRNA, even mRNA vaccines). Translation is the *payoff* of HS-LS1-1 — it's where DNA structure finally becomes protein — so leaving it deck-less is the chapter's real gap. Either add an 8.5 deck + CFU, or make the reliance on `IWS 8.5` explicit in the lesson plan. **The assessment is currently ahead of the teaching materials.**

2. **The 8.1 experiments are the best CER in the chapter — and the content is all there.** Griffith → Avery → Hershey-Chase is a classic "argue from evidence" sequence. CFU 8.1 only asks recall ("what did Griffith call it?"). One **[short-answer]** addition — *"Using the three experiments as evidence, construct an argument for why DNA, not protein, is the genetic material"* — converts the chapter's richest content into the HS-LS1-1/SEP task, with `IWS 8.1` as the evidence text.

3. **DNA modeling is equipped (8.2/8.3).** Structure and replication are taught on slides, but the **Carolina DNA Necklace Kit** and **Molecular Model Kit** (§7) are on hand. Building a base-paired strand and walking replication is the natural HS-LS1-1 hands-on, and the base-pairing CFU items (8.2 Q9, 8.3 Q3) already set it up.

4. **The Chapter 8 test was an unnamed "Untitled document" — now renamed.** It's a complete 22-question MCQ + answer key (the digital-workflow assessment), renamed this pass to `Chapter 8 Test.docx`. It's the right home for the one `[short-answer]` CER from finding #2.
