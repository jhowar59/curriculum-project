**A CER bolt-on and a CFU revision are the same artifact** — the question content — not a separate worksheet. CFUs are delivered in **two modes, chosen day to day**: handed out as the **paper worksheets** seen here, or **LLM-generated from the notes into a Google Form** (Forms/Classroom). The **[auto-grade]** / **[short-answer]** tags below matter **only in Forms mode**: [auto-grade] = recall item Forms scores automatically, [short-answer] = open CER/explanation item Forms can't grade. On paper every item is hand-graded anyway, so adding the CER item costs nothing extra. Going digital, the pattern is mostly [auto-grade] recall **+ one [short-answer] CER item**; on paper, just add that CER item.

Chapter 10 is the evolution core: early ideas and the groundwork in geology (10.1), Darwin's observations on the *Beagle*/Galápagos (10.2), the theory of natural selection (10.3), the classic lines of evidence — biogeography, embryology, comparative anatomy (10.4), and the modern molecular evidence (10.5). It anchors the **HS-LS4** cluster, most strongly **HS-LS4-1** (multiple lines of empirical evidence for common ancestry) and **HS-LS4-2 / HS-LS4-4** (the factors of natural selection → adaptation). **HS-LS4-3** (mathematical models of trait proportions) and **HS-LS4-5** (speciation/extinction) are expected in **Chapter 11** (population genetics) — flagged below as handoffs to verify when Ch11 is audited.

> **Note — mutation gap already addressed.** The chapter teaches "variation" only as a given ("natural selection acts on *existing* variations") and never sources it in mutation, leaving **HS-LS4-2**'s mutation factor and **HS-LS3-2**'s mutation prong short. A drop-in fix is committed alongside this card: **`10.3 Mutation Insert (DRAFT).md`** — two slide-ready slides to seat between 10.3 slides 7 and 9, plus a `CFU 10.3 - C` matching the existing A/B format. **Chapter verdict:** Coverage of the evolution standards Chapter 10 is responsible for is strong — **HS-LS4-1 is genuinely well-served**, with five or six independent lines of evidence (biogeography, embryology, comparative anatomy, vestigial structures, fossils/transitional forms, molecular/DNA, pseudogenes, Hox genes). Natural selection (HS-LS4-2/4-4) is solid conceptually, and the 10.3 CFUs already nudge toward reasoning. The chapter's weaknesses are specific and mostly handoffs: it's entirely **qualitative** (no math — HS-LS4-3 lives in Ch11), the mutation source of variation is missing (fix already drafted), and the version/edition clutter here is the heaviest of any chapter so far.

**Findings worth acting on:**

1. **HS-LS4-1 is begging for one synthesis argument — the evidence is all here, taught as recall.** The chapter lays out multiple independent lines of evidence across 10.4 and 10.5 but only ever asks students to define each in isolation (CFU 10.4/10.5). The standard is literally "analyze and interpret … *multiple lines* of empirical evidence." One **[short-answer]** item — *"Pick three different kinds of evidence (e.g. a homologous structure, a transitional fossil, a shared DNA/pseudogene sequence) and explain how, together, they support common ancestry"* — turns the chapter's biggest asset into the HS-LS4-1 task. Add it to the cumulative review or as a capstone CER.

2. **The mutation gap — already fixed, just needs converting.** See the note up top: `10.3 Mutation Insert (DRAFT).md` supplies the two slides + `CFU 10.3 - C`. Closes HS-LS4-2's mutation factor and HS-LS3-2's mutation prong. Remaining step is yours (slides → PowerPoint; the `- C` block → docx).

3. **Comparative anatomy is an equipped, on-topic lab — and there's no lab in the chapter.** 10.4's homologous-structures evidence is taught on slides, but the **Carolina Comparative Skeletal Anatomy Kit (§7)**, the **Comparative Skeletal Anatomy set + skeleton model (§8)**, and the preserved/skeletal specimens (§8) are all on hand. Comparing limb bones across species (human/cat/bird/whale) is a hands-on HS-LS4-1 evidence activity that needs nothing bought. A bird-beak/predation **natural-selection simulation** (craft consumables, §11) would similarly give 10.3 a data-generating activity.

4. **HS-LS4-3 (mathematical models) and HS-LS4-5 (speciation/extinction) are absent here — confirm they land in Chapter 11.** Chapter 10 is entirely qualitative; it never models how an advantageous trait's *proportion* shifts over generations (HS-LS4-3) and doesn't develop speciation as a process (HS-LS4-5). Both are standard **Chapter 11 (population genetics / Hardy-Weinberg)** material. This is a **handoff, not a defect** — but it's the thing to verify when Ch11 is audited, because between them they're three of the six HS-LS4 PEs.
